The BunBurner Rides and Rules

BunBurner rides are only available to IBA members who have completed a certified SS1000/SS1600K.

The ride protocols described in the Rider Guidance document and the Ride Documentation pack must be strictly applied.

The BunBurner series of rides consists of:

BunBurner 1500 (BB1500) or BunBurner 2500K (BB2500K)

1500 miles/2500kms in 36 hours

BunBurner 1500 SILVER (BBS) or BunBurner 2500K SILVER (BBS2500K)

1500 miles/2500kms in 30 hours

BunBurner GOLD (BBG) or BunBurner 2500K GOLD (BBG2500K).

1500 miles/2500kms in 24 hours

The SILVER/GOLD rides are extreme rides and it is expected that riders attempting these rides have already completed at least an SS1000 or SS1600K.

BunBurner GOLD 3000 (BBG3000) or BunBurner GOLD 5000K (BBG5000K)

It is expected that riders attempting these rides have already completed at least BBG, SS2000 or BBG2500K. Other rides may qualify and advice is available from verifications@ironbutt.co.uk

The BBG3000 is 2 consecutive BBG (1500 mile) rides in 48 hours, with the first leg of 1500 miles being completed in 24 hours and the second leg completed in the second 24 hour period, in no more than 48 hours over all. It is important to note that time left over from the first leg may not be used to lengthen the time for the second leg.

The mid-point between the 2 legs should be carefully considered. The ending time of the first leg may be used as the starting time of the second leg, but this sacrifices the remaining time of the first leg which may be used for rest. A rider may also rest beyond the maximum time for the first leg but the clock for the second leg AUTOMATICALLY starts no later than 24hours after the start of the first leg, irrespective of whether the rider is actually on the road or not.

Please Note: Riders wishing to claim triple BBGs should first seek advice from verifications@ironbutt.co.uk

BUNBURNER RULES

The basic rules are the same as for other IBA UK rides:

1. Ride the designated distance in the designated time (see below) on a safe route of your choice.

2. IBAUK requires that you document your ride with photographs. The photo must show BOTH the receipt AND your bike's odometer (mileage) reading IN THE SAME PHOTO. Receipts and odo readings must be legible. Record the Start and End details on Ride Documentation 2 and Ride Documentation 3 respectively.

3. Collect fuel and other receipts along your route and photograph each against your odometer. Track them, along with rest stops over 30 minutes, on Ride Documentation 4.

4. Complete all the remaining documentation in the Ride Documentation pack.

5. Copy all completed documentation and receipts and submit the copies, with your name on every sheet.

Additional Rules

BunBurner 1500 (BB1500K) or BunBurner 2500K (BB2500K)

1. Ride 1500 miles (2500km) in less than 36 hours.

BunBurner SILVER (BBS) or BunBurner 2500K SILVER (BBS2500K)

1. Ride 1500 miles (2500km) in less than 30 hours

BunBurner GOLD (BBG) or BunBurner 2500K GOLD (BBG2500K)

1. Ride 1500 miles (2500km) in less than 24 hours

BunBurner GOLD 3000 (BBG3000) or BunBurner GOLD 5000K (BBG5000K)

1. Ride two BBG rides back-to-back, i.e. 3000 miles or 5000km in 48 hours.

2. Each leg must be completed start to finish in 24 hours.

3. A petrol receipt or ATM receipt with computer recorded time is required to start leg 1.

4. A petrol, ATM or hotel receipt with computer recorded time is required to end the first leg.

5. The beginning of the second leg officially starts either at the time shown on the first receipt of the second leg or when the clock runs out on the first leg, whichever comes first.

6. In either case, a petrol or ATM receipt with computer recorded time is required to show the time the rider is back on the road after rest.

7. A petrol receipt or ATM receipt with computer recorded time is required to show the end of the second leg, thereby finalising the time for the BBG3000 within 48 hours.
